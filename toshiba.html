Associate Mobile Redesign

Toshiba GCS helps retailers streamline in-store operations. Its mobile technology empowers associates to deliver a fast, seamless shopping experience.

Problem
Store associates needed a faster, more reliable mobile tool to assist customers during peak hours. The old app caused delays, errors, and missed notifications, frustrating both staff and shoppers.
Role
Product Designer
Tools
Figma
Team
1 Product Designer, 1 Developer, 1 PM
View Product
UNDERSTANDING USER

Deep dive into users day to day

Conducted user interviews in collaboration with UX researcher with shopper assistants to understand their goals, challenges, and the nature of their daily work.

Interview

60 minutes virtual interview on zoom, recoreded and transcribed.

Research Goal

What challenges do store associates face when using the mobile tool during peak hours?

How do they use associate mobile app in their task

DEFINING THE PROBLEM

Current Checkout User Flow

Collaborated with the Sr. UX Designer on a heuristic evaluation of the associate mobile app to identify and address usability issues early, ensuring a smoother experience for associates and setting the foundation for more effective user testing.

Usability Testing Hypothesis

If we streamline steps, standardize patterns, and improve feedback, shoppers will complete checkout faster with fewer errors.

Existing Design
Proposed Design

1. One entry point in navigation for the action palette

2. Added notification indications

Usability Testing Hypothesis for 1 and 2

We believe adding a notification indicator and consolidating tasks into kabab menu will result in clearer, faster navigation because we know users miss updates and get confused by multiple menus.

Conclusion

Streamlining steps and aligning patterns reduced errors, sped up checkout, and improved shopper confidence.
